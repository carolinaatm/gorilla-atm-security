Our Story

Built by Operators. For Operators.

We didn't build Gorilla ATM Security in a boardroom. We built it after watching our own machines get hit — and deciding that the market needed something better.

The Origin Story

Why We Started in 2013

Gorilla ATM Security was born in the Washington, D.C. metro area in 2013. Our founders weren't security manufacturers or engineers by trade — they were independent ATM operators who'd been in the business for years and had built up a portfolio of machines across the region.

Then came a rash of break-ins. Multiple machines hit in a short period. Thousands of dollars in cash losses, damaged equipment, and the operational headache of getting machines back online. They looked at the market for a real solution and found that most enclosures were either too expensive for independent operators or weren't built tough enough to actually stop a motivated thief.

So they built their own. Starting with the problems they'd experienced firsthand — smash-and-grab, hook-and-chain, and pry attacks — they designed enclosures with one goal: to make it so difficult and time-consuming to break into a machine that thieves would move on. Heavy gauge steel. Serious locks. Real weight.

It worked. Word spread. Other operators wanted the same protection. Gorilla ATM Security was officially open for business.
